The LostData project is everything in one. It is a sketch pad, anadventure, a blog, a database, a learning space, a dream catcher, and everything in between. It is the sandals of a data nomad. LostData is the systems door that I religiously open when I arrive in the office at 5:30am every morning, when crickets are deep asleep and all is quiet. In that moment of tranquility, I connect with data, analyse and brew more coffee. I discover stories hidden inside rows of data. I see emotion and interactivity bubble in records of resilient people in Galgaduud, in Lower Shabelle and many diverse places that our beneficiaries call home. At noon, I create Tableau Dashboards, Story points and gnaw at my groundnuts. When I am not hunting for open data and painting dashboards, I rant, roll on the ground, peddle my feet in the air, and rant more about data.
